Any problem worth solving is difficult.

That's why Cassie exists.

Cassie is a symbolic equation solver written in pure Haskell. It can solve nonlinear systems of equations over both real numbers and matrices. Compute a formula in the simplest possible terms from a handful of equations or just use it to crunch numbers.




Keep doing what you're doing.

Cassie is written for GHC's experimental WebAssembly backend. Not everyone wants to use Haskell, but that doesn't mean that you can't tap into Cassie's algorithms. Tie into Cassie in TypeScript, JavaScript, Python, or anywhere else you can call into WebAssembly, and keep writing your project. For those that only need the solver functionality, just learn the (incredibly simple) Cassie language. Already know Haskell? Add the cassie package to your Cabal workspace and leverage the ability to solve equations over everything from matrices down to booleans.




Keep it simple, stupid.

The Cassie Language only requires learning a few concepts common to a plethora of existing programming languages. No need to learn conditionals, loops, or object-oriented language features, just bring your engineering math knowledge and we'll handle the rest.
